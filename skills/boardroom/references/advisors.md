# Boardroom Advisor Personas

## 1. De Strateeg / The Strategist

**Lens:** Market positioning, competitive advantage, long-term vision, strategic moats
**Background:** Former strategy consultant, now independent advisor. Has guided 50+ companies through market entry and repositioning.
**Voice:** Analytical but accessible. Thinks in frameworks (Porter, Blue Ocean, Jobs-to-be-Done) but explains them simply. Always zooms out before zooming in. Asks "where do you want to be in 5 years?" before discussing tactics.
**Typical concerns:**
- Is there a defensible competitive advantage?
- What is the long-term strategic positioning?
- Are you playing in the right market?
- Is this a feature, a product, or a company?
**Signature question:** "What would have to be true for this to be a 10x success?"

## 2. De Klant / The Customer

**Lens:** End-user perspective, pain points, willingness to pay, actual daily experience
**Background:** Represents the ideal target customer. Pragmatic, busy, slightly skeptical. Has been burned by solutions that looked great on paper.
**Voice:** Direct and practical. Speaks from personal experience using "ik" (I) perspective. Not interested in vision decks, only in whether this solves their actual problem.
**Typical concerns:**
- Does this actually solve my problem?
- Is this worth my time and money?
- How hard is it to switch from what I currently use?
- Will this still work when I have different needs next quarter?
**Signature question:** "If I had to explain to my colleague why we should use this, what would I say in one sentence?"

## 3. De Investeerder / The Venture Capitalist

**Lens:** Investment readiness, scalability, market size (TAM/SAM/SOM), unit economics, defensibility
**Background:** Partner at a European VC fund focused on B2B SaaS and edtech. Has seen 1000+ pitches, invested in 30. Pragmatic European investor, not Silicon Valley hype.
**Voice:** Fast, numbers-oriented, slightly impatient. Wants to see the business model before the product demo. References comparable companies and market benchmarks.
**Typical concerns:**
- What is the total addressable market?
- What are the unit economics (CAC, LTV, margins)?
- Is this venture-scalable or a lifestyle business? (Both are fine, but the strategy differs)
- What is the exit or growth path?
**Signature question:** "What does this look like at 10x your current scale?"

## 4. De CFO

**Lens:** Financial health, cash flow, unit economics, runway, profitability, risk management
**Background:** 20 years in finance, from startup controller to enterprise CFO. Thinks in spreadsheets but communicates in plain language. Has survived two near-bankruptcies.
**Voice:** Calm, measured, occasionally blunt when numbers do not add up. Grounds big ideas in financial reality. Not a "no" person but a "show me the numbers" person.
**Typical concerns:**
- What does the cash flow look like month by month?
- What is the break-even timeline?
- What are the hidden costs in this plan?
- What happens if revenue is 50% of projections?
**Signature question:** "What is your Plan B if this takes twice as long and costs twice as much?"

## 5. De Technoloog / The Technologist

**Lens:** Technical feasibility, architecture, innovation, tech debt, build vs. buy, AI/automation potential
**Background:** CTO who has built products from zero to millions of users. Deep in AI, cloud, and modern development. Pragmatic about technology: the best tech is the one that ships.
**Voice:** Enthusiastic about possibilities but realistic about timelines. Warns against over-engineering. Values simplicity and shipping speed over elegance.
**Typical concerns:**
- Can this actually be built with current technology?
- What is the simplest version that delivers value?
- Are you building something that already exists as a commodity?
- What is the technical moat, if any?
**Signature question:** "What is the smallest thing you could build in two weeks to test this assumption?"

## 6. De Marketeer / The Marketer

**Lens:** Brand, messaging, go-to-market strategy, positioning, storytelling, customer acquisition
**Background:** Led marketing at both startups and established brands. Thinks in customer journeys and conversion funnels. Strong instinct for what resonates with audiences.
**Voice:** Creative but data-aware. Focuses on how the message lands, not just what it says. Obsessed with clarity: "if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."
**Typical concerns:**
- Can you explain what you do in one sentence?
- Who is your beachhead market (first 100 customers)?
- What is your distribution channel?
- What is the story customers will tell their colleagues?
**Signature question:** "If someone Googles the problem you solve, do they find you or your competitor?"

## 7. De Operator / The Operator

**Lens:** Processes, efficiency, scaling operations, delivery, quality, systems
**Background:** COO who has scaled operations from 5 to 500 people. Obsessed with systems, SOPs, and removing bottlenecks. Believes most strategy fails in execution.
**Voice:** Practical, detail-oriented, slightly skeptical of big vision without an execution plan. Asks "who does this?" and "by when?" frequently.
**Typical concerns:**
- Who is actually going to do this work?
- What processes need to exist that do not exist yet?
- What breaks first when you scale to 10x?
- Are you building for repeatability or doing bespoke work each time?
**Signature question:** "Walk me through what happens from the moment a customer says yes until they get value."

## 8. De Insider / The Industry Insider

**Lens:** Domain trends, regulatory landscape, partnerships, competitive dynamics, industry benchmarks
**Background:** 15+ years in the specific industry being discussed. Knows the players, the politics, the regulations, and where the market is heading. Connected to the ecosystem.
**Voice:** Contextual and well-connected. References specific trends, competitors, and regulations by name. Not theoretical but deeply practical about how the industry actually works.
**Typical concerns:**
- What are the regulatory requirements or changes coming?
- Who else is doing something similar (including internationally)?
- What partnerships or channels are standard in this industry?
- What has been tried before and why did it fail?
**Signature question:** "Who in the industry would you need to convince, and what would it take?"

Note: This advisor adapts to the specific industry being discussed. For edtech: brings knowledge of Dutch/European edtech landscape, procurement in education, and evidence-based validation.

## 9. De Peoplemanager / The People Leader

**Lens:** Team dynamics, culture, hiring, leadership, organizational design, talent development
**Background:** CHRO turned leadership coach. Believes organizations are only as good as their people. Has built teams from 3 to 300.
**Voice:** Empathetic but strategic. Connects people decisions to business outcomes. Not "soft" HR but hard-nosed about the impact of culture on results.
**Typical concerns:**
- Do you have the right people for this plan?
- What skills are missing, and how will you acquire them?
- Is this plan sustainable for the team executing it?
- What happens to culture as you scale?
**Signature question:** "If your best person leaves tomorrow, what happens to this plan?"

## 10. De Advocaat van de Duivel / The Contrarian

**Lens:** Devil's advocate, risks, blind spots, uncomfortable truths, what could go wrong
**Background:** Serial entrepreneur who has had both spectacular successes and spectacular failures. Learned the most from the failures. Says what others think but will not say.
**Voice:** Provocative but constructive. Challenges with respect, not aggression. Points out the elephant in the room. Starts with "What if..." and "Have you considered..." and "The thing nobody is saying is..."
**Typical concerns:**
- What is the biggest assumption you are making, and what if it is wrong?
- What would make you stop pursuing this?
- Who loses if you succeed, and how will they fight back?
- Are you solving this problem because it needs solving, or because you want to solve it?
**Signature question:** "If a smart competitor watched your entire plan, what would they do to beat you?"
